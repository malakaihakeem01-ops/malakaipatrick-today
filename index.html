Scorpios and the Color Yellow: A Theory

Everyone says Scorpios are dark. All black everything, mysterious, brooding, scary. And yeah — some of that's true. But I think we've been misread, and I think the color yellow proves it.

Yellow is the most Scorpio color there is. Think about it. It demands attention without asking for it. It's warm on the surface but stare at it long enough and it'll genuinely hurt your eyes. It shows up unexpected. Nobody sees yellow coming the way they see red or blue coming. That element of surprise is deeply, fundamentally Scorpio.

We're not dark because we love the dark. We're dark because we understand contrast. And nothing hits harder than yellow on black. That's not coincidence. That's us.

03 / Food
This week I made

Sub In A Tub

All the flavor of an Italian sub, none of the bread. Meat, cheese, peppers, olives — tossed in dressing and eaten straight from the container. Lazy genius.

10Mins
2Servings
★☆☆Difficulty
Italian No Cook Low Effort
Ingredients
  • Ham, sliced4 oz
  • Salami4 oz
  • Pepperoni2 oz
  • Provolone, chopped4 oz
  • Iceberg lettuce2 cups
  • Banana peppers¼ cup
  • Red onion, sliced¼ cup
  • Cherry tomatoes½ cup
  • Italian dressing3 tbsp
  • Red wine vinegar1 tbsp
  • Dried oregano1 tsp
Steps
  1. Chop all meats and cheese into bite-sized pieces and throw into a large bowl or container.
  2. Add shredded lettuce, banana peppers, onion, tomatoes, and olives on top.
  3. Drizzle Italian dressing and red wine vinegar. Sprinkle oregano.
  4. Toss everything together until evenly coated. Taste and adjust.
  5. Eat straight from the tub. That is the whole point.
